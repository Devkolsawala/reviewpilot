A 1-star review just landed on your Play Store listing. Your hands hover over the keyboard. You know you need to reply within 24 hours — review reply velocity is a measurable ranking signal in 2026 — but you cannot afford to sound robotic, defensive, or generic. And you have exactly 350 characters to work with, including spaces.

This post is a library of 50 Play Store review response examples you can adapt today. Every template stays under the 350-character limit, comes with a paraphrased review excerpt for context, and includes a one-line note on why it works. We've grouped them into seven categories — five-star praise, four-star, three-star, two-star, one-star bug rants, feature requests, and spam — plus five India-tone variants for apps with a Hindi-speaking user base.

If your reply hits the character ceiling, the Play Console truncates it silently mid-sentence. Use the [Play Store Character Counter](/tools/play-store-character-counter) while drafting — it shows live count, suggests shorter phrasing, and runs an AI polish pass in your chosen tone.

## Category 1: 5-star praise (10 templates)

Most developers ignore positive reviews. That's a mistake. Replying to 5-star reviews shows future users that you read every word, and it converts one-time downloaders into loyal advocates. Keep these short.

**Template 1.1** (84 chars)
> Review: "Best note-taking app I've used. Sync is instant. Worth every rupee."

Reply: Thanks Priya! Glad sync is working as expected. Try the new tag colors in v4.2.

*Why it works:* Uses their name, references their specific praise, and teases a feature.

**Template 1.2** (112 chars)
> Review: "Five stars. Switched from Notion and never looked back."

Reply: Welcome aboard — we built this for ex-Notion users frustrated with offline support. Stay tuned for v5: cross-device markdown.

*Why it works:* Validates their switch and signals roadmap.

**Template 1.3** (98 chars)
> Review: "Genuinely useful. I use it every morning."

Reply: Mornings are the hardest moments to build a habit around — thanks for sticking with us. If a feature would help, reply here.

*Why it works:* Empathetic, opens a feedback channel.

**Template 1.4** (76 chars)
> Review: "Love it. Replaced 3 other apps."

Reply: Replacing three apps is the highest compliment we get. Cheers — and tell a friend if you can.

*Why it works:* Reframes as social proof and includes a soft referral ask.

**Template 1.5** (105 chars)
> Review: "Fast, clean, no ads. Rare combination."

Reply: We never plan to add intrusive ads. The paid tier funds development — your subscription literally keeps the lights on.

*Why it works:* Reinforces brand promise and explains the business model.

**Template 1.6** (140 chars)
> Review: "Excellent battery optimization."

Reply: Battery was a six-month engineering rewrite that nobody outside the team noticed. Your review is the first one to call it out — thank you.

*Why it works:* Shows the human effort behind the work; users love this.

**Template 1.7** (89 chars)
> Review: "Customer support is top-tier."

Reply: That'll make Anjali's day — she handles support. Will pass this along. Glad we could help.

*Why it works:* Names a real person, makes support visible.

**Template 1.8** (102 chars)
> Review: "Loved the latest update."

Reply: The v3.8 update was driven by user requests like yours. If anything is missing in your workflow, drop us a note.

*Why it works:* Credits the user community.

**Template 1.9** (120 chars)
> Review: "Beautiful UI. Even better functionality."

Reply: Our designer Karthik will cry happy tears reading this. Functionality was the harder fight, so it means more — thanks.

*Why it works:* Real teammate, real emotion.

**Template 1.10** (97 chars)
> Review: "Works perfectly on my old Redmi Note 8."

Reply: Optimizing for mid-range devices is a hill we die on. Glad the Redmi runs smooth. v4 keeps the same footprint.

*Why it works:* Validates a specific device, signals values.

## Category 2: 4-star with minor issue (8 templates)

Four-star reviewers are your most valuable feedback channel — they like the app but spotted a real friction. Reply within 48 hours, acknowledge the specific issue, and ask for a follow-up if you can fix it.

**Template 2.1** (148 chars)
> Review: "Great app but the export to PDF is slow."

Reply: PDF export uses the system print pipeline, which is slow on Android 12+. We're moving to direct rendering in v4.3 (target: 4 weeks). Will speed up 5×.

**Template 2.2** (122 chars)
> Review: "Almost perfect — wish it had dark mode."

Reply: Dark mode shipped in v3.9 last week. If you're on auto-update, you already have it. Settings → Appearance → System.

**Template 2.3** (135 chars)
> Review: "Solid app, but crashes once a week."

Reply: One weekly crash is one too many. Could you share your device model and Android version? Email logs to support@app.com — we'll dig in.

**Template 2.4** (160 chars)
> Review: "4 stars — onboarding was confusing."

Reply: Fair. Onboarding tested well with power users but loses casual users in the first three screens. We're rebuilding it for v4.0 — would love your feedback when ready.

**Template 2.5** (118 chars)
> Review: "Good but the widget keeps freezing."

Reply: The widget bug is fixed in v3.10.2 (rolling out today). Reinstall the widget after updating — that clears the cached state.

**Template 2.6** (132 chars)
> Review: "Wish I could organize folders inside folders."

Reply: Nested folders are coming in v4.1 — drag and drop, unlimited depth. Beta opens in two weeks; reply here if you want early access.

**Template 2.7** (140 chars)
> Review: "Good app, but expensive at ₹299/mo."

Reply: Fair feedback. We launched an annual plan at ₹1,800 (effective ₹150/mo) — half the price for users who commit yearly. Linked in settings.

**Template 2.8** (105 chars)
> Review: "Almost 5 stars — missing iCloud sync."

Reply: We sync via Google Drive and Dropbox on Android. iCloud is iOS-only. Cross-device sync is solid through Drive.

## Category 3: 3-star mixed (6 templates)

Three-star reviews are unstable — they tip either way depending on your reply. Stay calm, acknowledge both the positive and the negative, and offer a concrete path forward.

**Template 3.1** (165 chars)
> Review: "The app works but the ads are too aggressive."

Reply: Hear you. The free tier shows ads every five screens — too many. We're cutting that to every twelve in v4.0. Or upgrade to ad-free for ₹99/mo (less than chai).

**Template 3.2** (148 chars)
> Review: "Decent app, but the AI suggestions are wrong half the time."

Reply: AI accuracy is around 70% on average — we know. Hit thumbs-down on bad suggestions and the model retrains weekly. Should improve in two updates.

**Template 3.3** (122 chars)
> Review: "I like it but battery drain is real."

Reply: Battery profile shipped in v3.11. Settings → Battery → Low Power keeps sync to once an hour. Drops drain by about 60%.

**Template 3.4** (135 chars)
> Review: "App is OK but support is slow."

Reply: Support response time has been three days lately — unacceptable. We hired two more agents this month. Email us again; we'll prioritize.

**Template 3.5** (158 chars)
> Review: "Three stars — works fine but doesn't sync to my watch."

Reply: Wear OS support is a known gap. We're building it for v4.2 — companion app, complications, the works. Sign up for beta access at app.com/wear.

**Template 3.6** (110 chars)
> Review: "Decent but I expected more for a paid app."

Reply: Fair — paid tier needs to clearly out-deliver free. What's missing for you? Reply here and we'll add it to the roadmap.

## Category 4: 2-star unhappy (6 templates)

Two-star reviews mean something specific went wrong. Don't be defensive. Acknowledge, apologize where warranted, and route them to a fix or to direct support.

**Template 4.1** (172 chars)
> Review: "Lost my data after the update."

Reply: Data loss is the worst possible outcome and we're sorry. Email support@app.com with your user ID — we keep 30-day backups server-side and can restore within an hour.

**Template 4.2** (148 chars)
> Review: "Subscription auto-renewed without warning."

Reply: Google sends renewal notices but the email goes to spam often. Cancel from Play Store → Subscriptions. We refund any renewal within seven days — email support.

**Template 4.3** (140 chars)
> Review: "Two stars — feature removed in latest update."

Reply: We deprecated the manual sort in v3.9 because 0.5% of users used it and it broke other features. If you used it daily, email us — we'll find a workaround.

**Template 4.4** (135 chars)
> Review: "Stopped working after Android 15 update."

Reply: Android 15 broke our notification permissions. Hot-fix is in review at Google right now (1–2 days). Watch the version number, then reinstall.

**Template 4.5** (158 chars)
> Review: "Two stars. App is good but UI is dated."

Reply: A full UI refresh is in the v5.0 design phase right now — Material You, dynamic theming, larger touch targets. Targeting June launch. Worth waiting for.

**Template 4.6** (122 chars)
> Review: "Crashes on launch."

Reply: That's a packaging bug we just identified. Hotfix ships within 24 hours. Until then, clear cache → relaunch usually works.

## Category 5: 1-star angry / bug report (10 templates)

This is where most developers panic and write something defensive. Don't. The 1-star reply is read by hundreds of prospective users deciding whether to install. Be calm, specific, and offer a fix. Apologize when you should.

**Template 5.1** (190 chars)
> Review: "Worst app ever. Stole my money."

Reply: I'm sorry — that's a terrible experience. We never want a user to feel scammed. Email support@app.com with your transaction ID and we'll process a full refund within 48 hours, no questions asked.

**Template 5.2** (165 chars)
> Review: "App crashes constantly. Useless."

Reply: Sorry for the frustration. Crashes spiked after the Android 15 update — we found the bug and the fix lands today. Could you share device model? We want to confirm.

**Template 5.3** (148 chars)
> Review: "Why does this app need contacts permission?"

Reply: We don't, anymore. v3.9 removed the contacts permission entirely. If you're on an older version, please update — your data was never sent to us.

**Template 5.4** (185 chars)
> Review: "Premium feature didn't unlock after payment."

Reply: That's a payment-sync bug we hit twice this year. Force-close the app, open Settings → Account → Restore Purchase. If it doesn't unlock in 30 sec, email support@app.com — we'll fix it.

**Template 5.5** (155 chars)
> Review: "Got logged out and lost everything."

Reply: Logout shouldn't lose data — that's a sync bug. Email us your account email; we recover from server-side backup within an hour. Sorry this happened.

**Template 5.6** (165 chars)
> Review: "1 star. Terrible customer service."

Reply: Sorry we let you down. Email aditya@app.com directly — that's me, the founder. I'll personally make sure your issue is fixed by end of day. We can do better.

**Template 5.7** (140 chars)
> Review: "Battery killer. Drains 30% overnight."

Reply: 30% overnight is way too much. Background sync is the culprit. Settings → Battery → Low Power kills it. The v4.0 update fixes this by default.

**Template 5.8** (160 chars)
> Review: "Doesn't work on my OnePlus."

Reply: OnePlus's aggressive battery optimization kills our sync. Settings → Battery → App management → app name → Don't optimize. Fixes the issue 100% of the time.

**Template 5.9** (172 chars)
> Review: "Charged me twice for the same subscription."

Reply: That's a Play Billing duplicate — annoying and we're sorry. Email support@app.com with both transaction IDs. Refund processed within 24 hours. We'll also credit you a free month.

**Template 5.10** (148 chars)
> Review: "Update broke everything. Reverting to old version."

Reply: We never want users rolling back. What broke specifically? Reply here or email support@app.com — fixing it is more important than the bad review.

## Category 7: Spam / off-topic / competitor (5 templates)

These reviews target the next reader more than the reviewer. Stay neutral, short, factual.

**Template 7.1** (135 chars)
> Review: "Use [Competitor X] instead, it's better!"

Reply: Glad you found a tool that works for you — different apps fit different workflows. Anyone reading this, our free tier is at app.com/free.

**Template 7.2** (110 chars)
> Review: "1 star. Bad app." (no detail)

Reply: Hard to improve without specifics — what broke? Reply here or email support@app.com. We read every message.

**Template 7.3** (148 chars)
> Review: Off-topic political rant.

Reply: We're reporting this review for being off-topic, per Play Store guidelines. Anyone with actual app feedback, please email support@app.com directly.

**Template 7.4** (122 chars)
> Review: "App stole my data and sold it!"

Reply: We don't sell user data — full audit on our privacy page at app.com/privacy. Independent SOC 2 audit available on request.

**Template 7.5** (140 chars)
> Review: Five-star review that's clearly fake/paid.

Reply: Thanks for the kind words — but we don't accept paid reviews. Anyone reading this, please leave honest feedback only. Truth helps us improve.

## How to use this library

Don't copy any of these word-for-word. Google's spam detection flags identical replies across your account within hours, and users notice templated language fast. The right way to use this list:

1. Find the template closest to the review type.
2. Personalize the first line with something specific from the review — a feature name, a device model, the user's name.
3. Keep the structure of the response but rewrite at least 30% of the language.
4. Paste it into the [free 350-character reply tool](/tools/play-store-character-counter) to verify length and run an AI polish pass.
